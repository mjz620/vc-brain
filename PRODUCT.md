# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary user is a venture-capital investor or analyst — a partner or associate deciding whether to deploy a $100K check, often within 24 hours of first contact with a founder. Their context is high-stakes and time-compressed: they are triaging many opportunities, cannot take any claim at face value, and are accountable to an investment committee for every recommendation. The job to be done is to move an opportunity from first signal to a defensible decision — sourcing, screening on three independent axes, evidence-backed diligence, and a memo they can act on — without weeks of manual work and without trusting numbers they can't verify. Success is a recommendation the analyst can bring to IC with confidence because every assertion behind it is traceable.

## Product Purpose

FirstSignal is an evidence-first sourcing and diligence engine that compresses the path from first signal to investment decision. It ingests founder and company data from heterogeneous public sources, resolves and scores it, runs a transparent reasoning pipeline (screening → diligence → decision), and produces an investment memo where every claim carries a trust tier and a link to its evidence. It exists because capital today flows through networks rather than merit and diligence takes weeks; the product's reason to exist is to make a fast decision that is also an honest one. Success looks like an analyst reaching a well-founded invest/pass/conditional call in a single day, with the reasoning fully auditable.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, and credible. The voice is that of a sharp analyst who states what is known plainly, flags what is not, and never hypes. It should read as institutional confidence without stiffness — Notion-approachable in its legibility, Bloomberg-deep in its analytical density. The register is editorial-terminal: a serif memo voice for the reasoning, tabular precision for the data. The interface earns trust by being exact and unsentimental; emotion, where it appears, is the quiet confidence of evidence that holds up.

## Anti-references

Not consumer fintech — no rounded pastel friendliness, playful illustrations, or Robinhood/Cash-App softness; this is investment-committee work, not a spending app. Not enterprise legacy — no cluttered toolbars, dated corporate blues, heavy chrome, or low information-to-pixel ratio in the Salesforce mold. Not generic SaaS/AI-dashboard slop — no purple-blue AI gradients, gradient hero cards, identical icon-card grids, or the big-number KPI template. The design should never look like it was assembled from a component-library starter theme.

## Design Principles

Evidence over assertion. Every number, claim, and score exists to be checked; the interface's core job is to keep the trace from conclusion to raw source one click away, never buried.

Honest about what it knows. Gaps, contradictions, and confidence levels are foregrounded, not smoothed over. A flagged gap or a surfaced contradiction is a feature of the surface, not an error state to hide — the design gives them weight.

Depth without noise. Bloomberg-level information served at Notion-level legibility. Density is earned by the task; it is never decoration, and the three screening axes are shown side by side, never blended into one reassuring number.

The tool disappears into the decision. The analyst is in a 24-hour workflow, not admiring the UI. Speed, clarity, and a legible pipeline beat choreography; motion conveys state, never spectacle.

Precision as trust. Calm, exact, and consistent screen to screen. The same vocabulary, the same trust-tier language everywhere, so the analyst learns the system once and then stops thinking about it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA: body and data text meet AA contrast against their surfaces in both light and dark themes, and interactive elements are keyboard-reachable with visible focus. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` with a crossfade or instant alternative for every transition. The trust-tier and stance colors must not rely on hue alone — they carry a label or shape as well — so the corroboration tiers remain distinguishable for color-blind users and in high-glare demo conditions.
